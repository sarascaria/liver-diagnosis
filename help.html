SYMPTOMS WITH DISCRIPTION

1.Diabetes mellitus: Disorder in which blood sugar (glucose) levels are abnormally high because the body does not produce enough insulin to meet its needs.
2.Jaundice: Yellow staining of the skin and sclerae (the whites of the eyes) by abnormally high blood levels of the bile pigment bilirubin. The yellowing extends to other tissues and body fluids
3.Acitis:The accumulation of fluid in the peritoneal cavity, causing abdominal swelling.
4.Hepatic encephalopathy: it is a spectrum of neuropsychiatric abnormalities in patients with liver dysfunction, after exclusion of brain disease. [1, 2, 3] Hepatic encephalopathy is characterized by personality changes, intellectual impairment, and a depressed level of consciousness.
